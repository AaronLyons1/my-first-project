How to change a tyre

Locate the jack, usually stored in boot of car
locate tool kit also found in the boot of the car
Before jacking the car up, you need to crack the
wheel nuts. To do this place the on a tyre nut roughly 90° to the ground and push anti-clockwise. when all the nuts make a *crack* noice, jack the car into the air.
Find jack points on car, look underneath car usually flat spot underneath the car at the doors.
Once car in the air, fully take off wheel nuts followed by the wheel.
(note: wheel might need to be pulled off, be careful not to pull car off the jack. )

How to put on a spare tyre

Complete steps above before starting here
Find spare tyre, usually underneath boot cover or underneath back of car
Place wheel on wheel hub and line up the holes for the bolts
When all the bolts are in the holes and slightly tightened lower car
When car is on the ground further tighten the bolts until u cant anymore
Go straight to a garage and get a new tyre for your wheel.

How to Check Your Oil

First pop your bonnet, to do this find the tab in your car located at driver or passenger foot well.
Find the pull tab for your oil, it looks like a ring usually colored yellow or grey
When you pull it out with the oil off the metal.
Dip the rod the entire way back in again and pull it out, this insures an acurate reading
There are two marks on the oil, top means full bottom means empty, if your oil isnt near the top it might need to be topped up or changed.
